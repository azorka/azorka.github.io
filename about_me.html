About Me

Dr. Angela Szesciorka is a NSF Office of Polar Programs Postdoctoral Fellow in the Marine Mammal Institute at Oregon State Universityâ€™s Hatfield Marine Science Center. She earned her Ph.D. in oceanography as a Dr. Nancy Foster Scholar from Scripps Institution of Oceanography in 2021. She has been studying the movement and behavior of baleen whales off the US West Coast for more than nine years and now focuses on Arctic and sub-Arctic species. Dr. Szesciorka is interested in whale migration, foraging, and mating-related calling behavior as well as anthropogenic threats, such as ship strike risk, and how climate change is impacting whale ecology. She earned her master's in marine science from Moss Landing Marine Laboratories and her bachelor's degree in journalism from Duquesne University. When she's not in the field or analyzing data, Dr. Szesciorka can be found tidepooling in Half Moon Bay, riding roller coasters in Santa Cruz, or going to baseball games.
