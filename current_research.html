Current Research

Bowhead whale migration timing

As the Arctic warms disproportionately compared to the rest of the planet, there is evidence some bowhead whales might be overwintering on what is normally their summer feeding grounds in the Canadian Beaufort Sea. This provides whales with options when making migratory decisions in response to climate-induced shifts in the ecosystem. However, as the historic extent of sea ice shrinks, increased vessel traffic through the Bering Strait and high Arctic will increase the risk of vessel strikes for migrating whales. This research will explore relationships among whale presence, oceanography, and prey in the Bering, Chukchi, and Beaufort Seas; determine if migration patterns are changing; and assess the potential seasonal overlap between bowhead whales and vessel traffic.

Bowhead whale song diversity

From late Oct/early Nov into Apr/early May (encompassing the fall/spring migrations to/from the Bering Sea), the Bering-Chukchi-Beaufort population of bowhead whales produce structured call sequences repeated as song. These songs, which can last for hours and are thought to be produced by males as a reproductive strategy, generally consist of one to three phrase types composed of FM and AM, harmonically rich, broadband units (50-5000 Hz). Bowhead whales can also produce two distinct, harmonically unrelated sounds simultaneously. Bowhead whales have extremely diverse songs in populations found in the northeast Atlantic; however, the composition and diversity of bowhead whale song in the Bering Sea have yet to be described. In this project we aim too assess within and among year diversity in song.
