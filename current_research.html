Current Research

Bowhead whale migration timing

As the Arctic warms disproportionately compared to the rest of the planet, there is evidence some bowhead whales might be overwintering on what is normally their summer feeding grounds in the Canadian Beaufort Sea. This provides whales with options when making migratory decisions in response to climate-induced shifts in the ecosystem. However, as the historic extent of sea ice shrinks, increased vessel traffic through the Bering Strait and high Arctic will increase the risk of vessel strikes for migrating whales. This research will explore relationships among whale presence, oceanography, and prey in the Bering, Chukchi, and Beaufort Seas; determine if migration patterns are changing; and assess the potential seasonal overlap between bowhead whales and vessel traffic.
